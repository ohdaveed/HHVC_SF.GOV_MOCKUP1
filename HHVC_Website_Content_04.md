## Excessive Materials

**URL:** sf.gov/excessive-materials-building

### Title
Excessive materials

### Legal Reference
Sec 581(b)(18)

### Subtitle
Learn what excessive materials look like, what to do now, and when to report the issue.

### Signs of a Problem
- Clutter blocks walkways or doors
- Items are stacked against most walls
- Pathways or exits are hard to use safely

### What to Do Now
- Remove items you do not need
- Keep floors, doors, and exits clear
- Leave space near walls and baseboards for inspection and treatment

### Responsibility
**Tenant:**
- Keep the unit clear enough for inspection
- Reduce clutter so pest control can reach problem areas

**Property Owner:**
- Notify tenants in writing about required corrections
- Give tenants a reasonable timeline to fix the issue

### What Happens Next
HHVC may inspect the property. If HHVC finds violations, the owner or tenant must fix them.

### FAQ
**Q: Who is responsible for fixing this?**
A: Owners must keep housing safe and healthy. Tenant and owner responsibilities can vary by lease and by the type of issue.

**Q: Why does the problem keep coming back?**
A: Problems return when clutter, leaks, food sources, or access points are not fully addressed.

## Unsanitary Conditions

**URL:** sf.gov/unsanitary-conditions-building

### Title
Unsanitary conditions

### Legal Reference
Sec 581(b)(4)

### Subtitle
Learn what unsanitary conditions look like, what to do now, and when to report the issue.

### Signs of a Problem
- Trash, dirt, or debris builds up
- Surfaces stay dirty over time
- Strong odors continue from uncleaned areas

### What to Do Now
- Clean and disinfect affected areas
- Remove trash and contaminated materials
- Tell the property owner about conditions they must fix

### Responsibility
**Tenant:**
- Keep the unit reasonably clean
- Report leaks or plumbing issues quickly

**Property Owner:**
- Keep shared areas clean and sanitary
- Fix building problems that cause recurring unsanitary conditions

### What Happens Next
HHVC may inspect the property. If HHVC finds violations, the owner or tenant must fix them.

### FAQ
**Q: Who is responsible for fixing this?**
A: Owners must keep housing safe and healthy. Tenant and owner responsibilities can vary by lease and by the type of issue.

**Q: Why does the problem keep coming back?**
A: Conditions usually return when the root cause is not fixed, such as leaks, poor trash handling, or ongoing sanitation gaps.

## Report a Pest or Sanitation Problem

**URL:** sf.gov/report-residential-building-concern

### Title
Report a pest or sanitation problem

### Subtitle
Use this form to report a housing health or sanitation problem to the City.

### When to Use This Page
Tell your property owner first.

Use this page when:
- You already told the owner or manager about the problem
- The problem was not fixed
- You need City inspection or enforcement help

### Before You Submit
Gather this information before you start:
- Property address
- Photos of the issue
- Dates the issue happened
- Notes or records showing when you contacted the owner or manager

### Primary CTA
- Go to the reporting form

### What Happens Next
1. SFDPH reviews your complaint.
2. An inspector may contact you and schedule a visit.
3. If violations are found, the owner will get a notice with required corrections.

## Vector Control and Healthy Housing Fee

**URL:** sf.gov/vector-control-healthy-housing-fee

### Title
Pay your Vector Control and Healthy Housing fee

### Subtitle
Owners of apartment and hotel properties in San Francisco must pay an annual fee. This fee supports the Vector Control and Healthy Housing Inspection Program.

### Before You Pay
Before you pay, have your property address, number of units, and billing information ready.

### Annual Cost Overview
The fee depends on building size. Use this page to confirm your amount and pay online.

| Building type | Number of units | Annual fee |
|---|---|---|
| Apartment / hotel | 1–4 units | Confirm with SFDPH |
| Apartment / hotel | 5–19 units | Confirm with SFDPH |
| Apartment / hotel | 20+ units | Confirm with SFDPH |

### Fee Lookup Steps
1. Find your property type
2. Count units
3. Confirm fee and pay online

### Primary CTA
Pay your fee online — Pay now

### What Happens Next
If you do not pay the fee, the City may add late fees or place a lien on your property.

### Need Help
Questions about your bill? Call (415) 252-3800 or email hhvc@sfdph.org.

### FAQ
**Q: Who is required to pay?**
A: Property owners of multi-unit residential buildings must pay the annual inspection program fee. The fee is based on the number of units at the property.

**Q: What if I believe I was billed incorrectly?**
A: Contact HHVC if you think your bill is wrong. Call (415) 252-3800 or email hhvc@sfdph.org with your property address and details about the issue.
