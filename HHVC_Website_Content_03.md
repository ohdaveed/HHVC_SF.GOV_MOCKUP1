## Poison Oak

**URL:** sf.gov/poison-oak-building

### Title
Poison oak

### Legal Reference
Sec 581(b)(11)

### Subtitle
Learn what this issue looks like, what to do now, and when to report it.

### Signs of a Problem
- Identifiable toxic plants in yard or lot
- Three-leaf clusters, waxy appearance
- Overgrowth in accessible areas

### What to Do Now
- Avoid all contact
- Do not attempt removal without protection
- Report to property manager

### Responsibility
**Tenant:**
- Avoid contact, report to owner

**Property Owner:**
- Remove safely or hire licensed professional
- Keep the plant from growing back where people can reach it

### What Happens Next
HHVC may inspect the property. If HHVC finds violations, the owner or tenant must fix them.

### FAQ
**Q: Who is responsible for fixing this?**
A: Property owners must keep housing safe and healthy. Tenant and owner responsibilities can vary by lease and by the type of issue.

**Q: Why does the problem keep coming back?**
A: The problem often returns when the root cause is not fixed, like leaks, food sources, clutter, or entry points.

## Mold Growth

**URL:** sf.gov/mold-growth-building

### Title
Mold growth

### Legal Reference
Sec 581(b)(6)

### Subtitle
Learn what this issue looks like, what to do now, and when to report it.

### Signs of a Problem
- Visible mold on walls, ceilings, or surfaces
- Musty odor in rooms
- Condensation on windows or cold walls
- Water stains or discoloration

### What to Do Now
- Report leaks to property owner immediately
- Improve ventilation in bathrooms and kitchens
- Wipe visible condensation promptly

### Responsibility
**Tenant:**
- Report leaks and moisture promptly
- Run exhaust fans, open windows

**Property Owner:**
- Repair all plumbing and building envelope leaks
- Improve ventilation where needed

### What Happens Next
HHVC may inspect the property. If HHVC finds violations, the owner or tenant must fix them.

### Partner Agency
**HHVC handles:**
- Visible mold and mildew conditions in housing
- Unsanitary conditions related to moisture and habitability

**Dept. of Building Inspection handles:**
- Broken pipes and plumbing system failures
- Faulty roofs or building defects causing water intrusion

If mold is caused by a broken pipe or faulty roof, report that structural issue to the Department of Building Inspection.
