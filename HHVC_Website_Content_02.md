## Bed Bugs

**URL:** sf.gov/bed-bugs-in-your-building

### Title
Bed bugs

### Subtitle
Learn how to identify bed bugs, avoid spreading them, and get help quickly.

### Signs of a Problem
- Bites in lines or clusters
- Blood spots on sheets
- Dark spotting on mattress seams
- Live bugs or shed skins

### Images
- What spotting can look like — Example of dark spotting near mattress seams and tags.
- Common signs near beds and furniture — Examples of shed skins and other signs around sleeping areas.

### Key Message
Keep furniture, mattresses, and bedding in place until you report the problem. Moving infested items can spread bed bugs to new areas.

### What to Do Now
- Report to your landlord right away by email, text, or letter
- Do not move items between rooms
- Contain items in sealed bags before disposal
- Wash and dry affected fabrics on high heat

### Responsibility
**Tenant:**
- Report promptly in writing
- Follow preparation instructions
- Provide access for treatment

**Property Owner:**
- Arrange professional treatment
- Coordinate building-wide inspection
- Provide prep instructions

### What Happens Next
If activity is confirmed, treatment and preparation steps will be required. The property owner is generally responsible for arranging pest control.

### How Bed Bugs Spread
- They move with luggage, clothing, and furniture.
- They can spread room to room through shared walls and items.
- Containment matters before treatment starts.

### What Treatment Usually Includes
- Inspection of sleeping areas and nearby furniture
- Preparation steps for the resident or owner
- Follow-up treatment if activity continues

### FAQ
**Q: Do I need to throw away my mattress?**
A: Usually no. Follow the preparation instructions you receive first.

**Q: Can bed bugs spread through walls?**
A: Yes. They can move through shared building spaces and items.

## Flies and Sanitation

**URL:** sf.gov/flies-sanitation-building

### Title
Flies and sanitation

### Legal Reference
Sec 581(b)(8)

### Subtitle
Learn how to identify this condition, what to do now, and how to report it if it is not being corrected.

### Signs of a Problem
- Large numbers of flies indoors or near garbage
- Maggots or larvae in trash or drains
- Fly spotting on walls or ceilings
- Strong odor from waste or refuse area

### What to Do Now
- Remove food waste and garbage promptly
- Use containers with tightly fitting lids
- Clean garbage bins including inside surfaces
- Remove breeding material — do not rely on fly strips

### Responsibility
**Tenant:**
- Bag and contain waste properly
- Close bin lids after use

**Property Owner:**
- Provide adequate containers with tight lids
- Maintain and clean refuse areas

### What Happens Next
HHVC may inspect the property. If HHVC finds violations, the owner or tenant must fix them.

### FAQ
**Q: Who is responsible for fixing this?**
A: Owners must keep living conditions safe and fix any issues. Responsibility varies with the lease and context.

**Q: Why does the problem keep coming back?**
A: Recurring activity often means root causes remain. These include entry points, moisture, food sources, and slow treatment.

## Mosquitoes and Standing Water

**URL:** sf.gov/mosquitoes-standing-water

### Title
Mosquitoes and standing water

### Subtitle
Learn how to find and eliminate mosquito breeding sources and when to report a problem.

### Signs of a Problem
- Standing water in containers or gutters
- Larvae visible in containers
- Clogged drains or pooled water
- Repeated mosquito activity

### What to Do Now
- Dump all standing water from containers weekly
- Empty saucers under potted plants
- Cover or remove unused water-holding containers
- Check and clear blocked gutters

### Images
- Containers that hold water — Examples of buckets, toys, and planters that collect water.
- Places to check after rain — Examples of gutters, drains, and low spots to inspect.

### Responsibility
**Resident:**
- Empty containers weekly
- Remove clutter from outdoor areas

**Property Owner:**
- Maintain drainage systems
- Correct drainage failures

### What Happens Next
Drainage failures that cannot be self-corrected may require property owner action following an inspection.

### Field Services Link
Still having a problem after eliminating standing water? HHVC can send a team to your property. Request a mosquito survey →

### Commonly Missed Breeding Sites
Check gutters, covered containers, saucers, and low points where water collects after rain.

### What to Do After Rain
Inspect the property within 24 hours and remove standing water from all containers and drainage points.

### FAQ
**Q: How much water does it take to breed mosquitoes?**
A: Very small amounts can be enough for breeding, so remove even small pools of standing water.

## Pigeons and Bird Waste

**URL:** sf.gov/pigeons-bird-waste-building

### Title
Pigeons and bird waste

### Legal Reference
Sec 581(b)(7)

### Subtitle
Learn how to identify this condition, what to do now, and how to report it if it is not being corrected.

### Signs of a Problem
- Accumulated droppings on ledges or roofs
- Active nesting or roosting on building
- Feathers in vents or common areas

### What to Do Now
- Do not feed pigeons or other birds
- Report waste accumulation to property manager

### Responsibility
**Tenant:**
- Stop feeding birds on the property
- Report nesting or waste to owner

**Property Owner:**
- Remove droppings and nesting safely
- Install lawful deterrence measures

### Key Message
Do not sweep or disturb large accumulations of dry droppings without respiratory protection. Wet the area first or contact a professional.

### What Happens Next

## Fleas

**URL:** sf.gov/fleas-in-your-building

### Title
Fleas

### Legal Reference
Sec 581(b)(8)

### Subtitle
Learn how to identify this condition, what to do now, and how to report it if it is not being corrected.

### Signs of a Problem
- Bites on ankles and lower legs
- Flea dirt in pet bedding or carpet
- Pets scratching or grooming excessively
- Jumping insects near floor level

### What to Do Now
- Treat pets with veterinarian-recommended product
- Vacuum all carpets and upholstered furniture
- Wash all pet bedding and fabrics
- Clean pet resting areas completely

### Responsibility
**Tenant:**
- Treat pet and environment simultaneously
- Vacuum repeatedly over 2–4 weeks

**Property Owner:**
- Provide pest-free unit to new tenants
- Address common-area infestations

### What Happens Next
HHVC may inspect the property. If HHVC finds violations, the owner or tenant must fix them.

### FAQ
**Q: Who is responsible for fixing this?**
A: Owners must keep living conditions safe and fix any issues. Responsibility varies with the lease and context.

**Q: Why does the problem keep coming back?**
